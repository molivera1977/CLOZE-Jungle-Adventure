🌴 Lost in the Amazon
A story about courage, curiosity, and the rainforest

Maya had always dreamed of exploring the Amazon rainforest. When her father, a scientist, invited her on his research trip to Brazil, she could hardly contain her excitement. She packed her notebook, her compass, and her favorite pair of binoculars.

On their first morning in the jungle, Maya woke before sunrise. The air was thick and humid, heavy with the smell of wet leaves and earth. Strange bird calls echoed through the trees as thin rays of sunlight began to filter through the thick canopy above.

As she followed a narrow trail, Maya noticed how diverse the rainforest was. In just a few steps, she counted three types of colorful frogs, two kinds of flowering vines, and a spider monkey watching her from high in the branches. She scribbled notes as fast as she could.

Then it happened. Maya turned around and realized she could no longer see the research camp. Her heart began to race, but she remembered what her father had taught her: stay calm, observe your surroundings, and trust your training. She looked for the sun to find her direction and listened for the sound of the nearby river.

After a tense twenty minutes, Maya heard voices and splashing water. She pushed through a wall of broad leaves and stepped into a clearing right next to the camp. Her father ran toward her, relieved. Maya grinned and held up her notebook — she had filled six whole pages with observations.

"I wasn't lost," she told her father with a smile. "I was just doing extra research."
